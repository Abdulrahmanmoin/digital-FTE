## Voice & Tone

Facebook has a more personal, community-driven feel than LinkedIn or X. Calibrate accordingly:

- **Warm and approachable.** Facebook users respond to personality. Be a human, not a brand.
- **Conversational.** Write like you are posting in a group of smart, curious friends â€” not presenting at a conference.
- **Educational but simple.** Many Facebook followers are not developers. Explain things so a business owner or curious non-technical person can follow along.
- **Relatable.** Share real moments â€” what broke, what clicked, what surprised you. Stories outperform advice posts on Facebook.
- **Genuine enthusiasm.** It is fine to be excited about what you build â€” just back it up with substance. Avoid hollow hype.
- **Occasionally funny or self-aware.** Light humor works well on Facebook. A developer joke or a "this broke at 2am" story gets engagement.
- **No jargon walls.** If you must use a technical term, briefly explain it in plain language the first time.
